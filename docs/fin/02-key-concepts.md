# Key concepts: organizations, Cargo, Galley, Manifest, Supply

## Organization (group)

An **organization** is Ration’s workspace: inventory, recipes, meal plan, supply list, and **AI credit balance** all belong to that group. You can belong to more than one organization; you pick an **active** one to work in (see *Switching groups*).

## Cargo

**Cargo** is your **pantry inventory**: items with quantity, unit, optional expiry, domain (food, household, alcohol), and tags. Adding items can trigger a **merge suggestion** when Ration thinks two names are the same product.

## Galley

The **Galley** is your **recipe library** (full recipes) and **provisions** (simple single-item foods). Ingredients can be linked to Cargo for accurate cook and shopping math.

## Manifest

The **Manifest** is the **meal plan calendar**: meals placed in breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack slots. You can mark meals consumed so ingredients deduct from Cargo.

## Supply

**Supply** is the **shopping list**, built from what your planned and selected meals need minus what you already have in Cargo. You can snooze items you do not need to buy and (on eligible plans) share a public link.

## Credits

**AI credits** are spent on operations like receipt scan, AI recipe generation, URL recipe import, and AI plan-week. They are stored on the **organization**, not on individual users—every member draws from the same pool.

**MCP** (AI assistant integration) **read/write tools do not consume AI credits**; they are separate from credit-gated web features.
